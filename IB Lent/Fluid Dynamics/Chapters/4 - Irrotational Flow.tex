\section{Velocity Potential}
We consider irrotational flow: $\nabla \times \vec{u} = \vec0$. Then we have the velocity potential $\phi$:
\begin{equation*}
    \vec{u} = \nabla \phi
\end{equation*}
Then the incompressibility condition $\nabla \cdot \vec{u} = 0$ implies that $\phi$ is harmonic. Therefore, we solve Laplace's equation on the given domain to find $\vec{u}$.
\section{Uniform Flow Past an Object}
\subsection{Flow Past a Sphere}
Consider fluid moving with speed $U$ in the $\vec{e_z}$ direction past a sphere of radius $a$.

We solve $\nabla^2 \phi = 0$ with spherical polar coordinates and require:
\begin{equation*}
    \phi(r, \theta) \to Ur\cos(\theta) \text{ as } r \to \infty,\qquad \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial r}(a, \theta) = 0
\end{equation*}
We find the solution:
\begin{equation*}
    \phi = U\cos(\theta) \left[r + \frac{a^3}{2r^2}\right]
\end{equation*}
\subsection{Flow Past a Cylinder with Circulation}
For the flow past a cylinder, we allow a circulation:
\begin{equation*}
    \oint_{B_r(\vec{0})} \vec{u} \cdot \vec{dx} = \kappa.
\end{equation*}
We solve $\nabla^2 \phi = 0$ in cylindrical polar coordinates along with $\phi \to Ur\cos(\theta)$ as $r \to \infty$ and $\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial r}(a, \theta) = 0$.

The solution is:
\begin{equation*}
    \phi = \frac{\kappa}{2\pi} \theta + U\cos(\theta) \left[r + \frac{a^2}{r}\right]
\end{equation*}
